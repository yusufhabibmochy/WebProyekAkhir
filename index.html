Background
Today, there are many scientists who have great ideas but have difficulty developing their ideas due to limited discussion with experts or financial problems. These young scientist are competing to develop their research results by participating in competitions.

Therefore, "Scientific Paper, Poster, Innovation & Invention Competition" or known as (Sci-PIIC) in-collaboration with Indonesian Young Scientist Association (IYSA) will hold an international-level innovation & invention competition as a platform to nurture our young talented scientist to help build the future.
Category

Invention & Innovatpion

Competition branch that focuses on encouraging young scientists to realize their idea and creativity to invent and innovate in the field of science.

Scientific Paper

A writing competition that aims to convey the creative ideas of young scientists through scientific research and literature review in solving the problems around them.

Poster

Poster Competition encourages students to convey their outstanding innovations and ideas in the form of a two-dimensional visual representation of a research summary that includes easy-to-follow graphs, tables, and pictures.

Requirement
  1. Participats is students (elementary, secondary, and university).
  2. Each team consist of maximum 5 students and to appoint 1 team leader
  3. All works submitted to Sci-PIIC must be originals that have never been published and have never won any other competitions.
  4. The title of the essay/scientific paper/poster must be in accordance with the 2022 Sci-PIIC theme and the content of the work is described and presented using the correct scientific writing format.
  5. All electronic submissions must be submitted online by the applicable deadline. Entries received after the deadline will not be accepted and are considered eliminated.
  6. The committee will have the right and authority to publish essays, scientific works, and posters while still including the authors' name on them.
  7. The decision of the jury is final and cannot be contested.
  8. Participants disqualified from competitions for unethical behavior, including but not limited to plagiarism or academic dishonesty, may be banned from competing in future events for a period of time determined by Sci-PIIC committee.
